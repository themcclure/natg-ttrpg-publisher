# Outpost 428 — Staff Areas

Staff-only or restricted-access locations within [[outpost-428]]. Public-accessible areas in [[outpost-428-public]].

---

## The Nexus

**Type:** Operations Center
**Phase:** Tower
**Access:** Shift personnel and above

The operational heart of Outpost 428, located in the tower section. The 360-degree view is real windows — actual transparent hull material, not screens. The asteroid field, docking traffic, and surrounding space are directly visible. On yellow or red alert, heavy shield screens drop automatically.

Shift stations ring the perimeter; the center floor is open. An open staircase leads up to the Director's Office — sound carries, and there is no private conversation on the floor. An express turbolift in the corner runs directly to the Docking Bay, bypassing main corridors.

---

## Director's Office

**Type:** Executive Office
**Phase:** Tower
**Access:** Director and invited personnel

Positioned near the top of the tower. Real windows overlook docking traffic and surrounding space. Open-plan and designed for working conversations, not ceremony. Accessible from The Nexus via an open staircase.

Portraits of all previous Directors line the office walls and the staircase down — every person who has held this posting since commissioning, in order. The first portrait is of a "retired" Starfleet captain.

---

## Briefing Room

**Type:** Briefing / Meeting Room
**Phase:** Tower
**Access:** Staff and cleared guests

Large formal briefing room adjacent to The Nexus. Central table, display screens, seating for a full shift plus guests. Used for pre-mission briefings, formal crew addresses, and meetings requiring more structure than the Nexus floor allows.

---

## Staff Commissary

**Type:** Staff Social / Dining Space
**Phase:** Phase 1/2 (original prefab, expanded)
**Access:** Station staff — all departments

The largest communal space in the staff areas. An open replimat — multiple terminals, no queue barriers — surrounded by social space with seating and tables. Shift change is loud here. Third shift at 0300 is quieter but never empty.

Food and drink from the replicator is free, drawn from the approved menu. The menu hasn't been meaningfully curated in decades — somewhere in the database are dishes that were fashionable in the 2320s and have not been ordered since. Removing an item requires a formal request to FORA. Nobody has bothered.

Fresh produce from the Agriculture Facility operates on a light barter system: volunteer time in exchange for access. Surplus from good harvests goes to communal celebrations.

---

## Hab Ring

**Type:** Residential and Diplomatic Zone
**Phase:** Phase 2/3 (the largest ring)
**Access:** Staff-restricted; honored guests by arrangement

The largest of the rings encircling the outpost's central column, and the one that feels most like a place people actually live. Quarters range from standard staff berthing to diplomatic suites. The ring connects to the [[persephone-agora]] via enclosed walkways — you can walk to the market from your bunk, which matters over a long rotation.

**Contains:** Staff quarters (Federation-standard), non-Federation staff quarters (adapted for atmospheric and environmental variance), visitor quarters (for trusted guests on extended stays), and diplomatic suites (for visiting dignitaries and high-level negotiations requiring privacy).

---

## Main Engineering

**Type:** Operations and Infrastructure Hub
**Phase:** Phase 1/2
**Access:** Engineering staff and authorised personnel

The operational engine room of Outpost 428. Louder than anything else on the station, lit differently — amber and white of active systems. Engineering runs continuously. There is always a shift here.

**Contains:** Repair Center (workshop and fabrication for equipment and hull components), Power Plant (primary energy generation; sustains post-scarcity replication and the transport interference field), and Waste Reclamation (collection and pre-processing; feeds into the Waste Processing Plant).

---

## Waste Processing Plant

**Type:** Utilities Infrastructure
**Phase:** Phase 2 (excavated interior)
**Access:** Maintenance staff only

The outpost's waste processing and recycling systems. Cramped access corridors, active processing units, poor ventilation. Almost never visited by anyone who doesn't have to be there.
