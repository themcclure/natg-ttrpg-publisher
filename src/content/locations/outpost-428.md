# Outpost 428

**Location type:** Outpost
**Type:** Asteroid-embedded facility (mixed surface/subsurface)
**Parent:** [[persephone-system]]
**Status:** Active
**Administered by:** Federation Office of Regional Administration
**Commanding officer:** [[gareth-desar]] (Director)

---

## Profile

Outpost 428 is built into and onto a large asteroid in the Persephone system, at the edge of Federation space. Approximately 3.6 light-years from Fenric's Star and ~85 days from Starbase 23 at warp 5. Communications with Command run on a dispatch model — no live subspace relay at this distance. The crew makes decisions before they can ask for guidance.

The outpost's mandate is diplomatic and trade: a neutral hub for commerce and brokered negotiation between the Federation and the sector's factions. It is a **Federation civilian facility**, not a Starfleet installation. The commanding officer holds the title of **Director**, not Commander. Starfleet personnel serve on secondment to support that mandate. The distinction matters politically — this is overtly a civilian trade and diplomatic post, and the sector factions came to the table on that basis.

Population: approximately 400–600, including commissioned crew, civilian contractors, FORA staff, and dependents. A non-trivial fraction were born here. This is a community, not just a posting.

## Architecture: Three Distinct Phases

The outpost's physical layout reflects its history. The three zones are visually and culturally distinct.

**Phase 1 — The Prefab Layer (surface, original):** The original structures dropped during initial claim. Functional, bolted to bare rock, largely unchanged. Some sections partially upgraded; others haven't been touched since commissioning. 30+ years old and it shows.

**Phase 1b — The Tower (surface, permanent build-out):** Added after the prefab claim was established. Houses The Nexus (operations center) and the Director's Office. Visible on approach — the most architecturally deliberate structure on the asteroid.

**Phase 2 — The Excavated Interior (subsurface, main facility):** The bulk of the outpost — carved into the asteroid after commissioning. Operations, diplomatic wing, science labs, residential decks, school, medical, and the entertainment facilities that make a long posting survivable. The aesthetic is dated Federation institutional: clean lines, slightly yellowed lighting panels.

**Phase 3 — The Docking and Cargo Platform (surface, external):** The outpost's public face — the part visiting ships see first. Larger and more recently maintained than Phase 1. Houses the docking bays, cargo processing, flight control, and sensor arrays.

## Command Structure

**Director:** Civilian appointment via FORA. Administrative lead for the outpost's mandate. The commanding officer.

**Shift operations:** Day-to-day running is divided across three shifts. Seniority runs oldest to newest: daytime = most experienced team, evening = mid-rotation, overnight = newest arrivals. The overnight shift leads have genuine autonomy and are the crew the Director can most easily deploy elsewhere.

**Officer of the Day (OD):** The overnight crew has no fixed internal hierarchy. Whoever is nominally in charge of the shift is the OD — a rotating designation. There is always an OD; their name is in the log.

**Permanent civilian chiefs** (Security, Engineering, Medical) do not rotate. They accumulate institutional knowledge that no rotation model can replicate.

## Operations

- **Flight operations:** 3 runabouts (one typically on SB23 supply rotation)
- **Trade and commerce:** Primary visible function
- **Diplomatic wing:** Ambassadorial suites for all sector factions — staffing levels are live political signals
- **Subspace communications:** Outside real-time range; messages run on narrative-necessity delay

## History

- **2347:** Federation claim established. Outpost commissioned as formal facility within weeks.
- **2347–2352:** First Director — a "retired" Starfleet captain; military oversight dressed as civilian administration during the outpost's volatile early years.
- **2352 onward:** Succession of actual diplomats on 5–6 year rotations.
- **2371:** Previous Director (joined Trill) recalled for Dominion diplomatic expertise. Compressed handover to Gareth Desar.
- **Early 2372:** Gareth Desar takes post.
- **2373–2375:** Dominion War. Gareth at post throughout.
- **Mid-2377:** Campaign begins. New overnight crew arrives.
