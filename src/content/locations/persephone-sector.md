# Persephone Sector

**Location type:** Sector
**Status:** Active

---

## Profile

A 20×20×20 light-year volume at the edge of Federation space, anchored by the Persephone system. The sector appears unremarkable on external cartography — most surveys record only a handful of notable systems — because the Persephone Geometry distorts long-range sensors and masks the actual population of 7+ systems within.

The sector sits approximately 50 light-years from Starbase 23, placing it firmly beyond real-time subspace communications range. The crew of Outpost 428 makes real-time decisions without a safety net.

The sector is characterized by contested political space: Federation border, pocket empires, independent worlds, and an aggressively isolationist system all coexist within warp-3 range of each other.

---

## The Persephone Geometry

A Class I planetary nebula remnant (collapsed, low-density) occupying a significant portion of the sector interior. It is not a hazard zone — the remnant is thin and diffuse. Its structural effect creates two navigable slipstream corridors that function like a tube network for warp travel. The warp bubble is maintained by the geometry rather than the engines, reducing engine strain and allowing higher effective speeds inside.

**Effects inside the Geometry:**
- Sensor tasks: Difficulty +3
- Engine/propulsion tasks (maintaining warp): Difficulty −1 (the geometry does the work)
- Travel inside the corridors is faster and smoother than open space at equivalent warp

**Entry points:**
- Path 1 (A-to-W corridor): Entry ~0.11 ly from Persephone, ~3 days at Warp 2 toward Fenric's Star
- Path 2 (W-to-B corridor): Entry ~0.22 ly from Varanidia, ~8 days at Warp 2 toward Fenric's Star

Both paths converge at Fenric's Star, making it the de facto junction. Whoever controls passage through the Fenric system influences access to both arms of the Geometry — which is why that system is diplomatically significant beyond its own population.

The Geometry substantially degrades long-range sensors throughout the sector interior. From outside, the sector appears to contain only a handful of notable systems. Plan accordingly, and do not assume your instruments are telling you the complete picture.
