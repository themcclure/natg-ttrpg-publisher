# Outpost 428 — Public Areas

Publicly accessible or semi-public locations within [[outpost-428]]. Staff-only areas in [[outpost-428-staff]].

The outpost supports a significant permanent population plus rotating visitors. Multiple bars and restaurants are distributed across the station, concentrated around the Persephone Agora — the areas below are documented; others exist and develop as they become relevant.

---

## Docking Bay

**Type:** Docking and Cargo Platform
**Phase:** Phase 3 (surface, external)

The outpost's public face — the first thing visitors see on approach. The bay is open to space, held pressurized by a force field. Capacity for up to 10 shuttles or runabouts. Larger vessels unable to enter the bay use two external hard docking points on the asteroid surface.

**Contains:** Traffic Control, Customs and Quarantine (all arrivals processed here), Transporter Room, Shuttle Bay (internal, houses the outpost's own runabouts).

> [!NOTE] A station-wide transport interference field is active at all times. All arrivals must use the customs transporter pads in the Docking Bay. Attempting to beam directly to any other location will result in interception and rerouting to Customs — with equipment processed separately. This is posted in approach protocols. Enforcement is automatic.

## Diplomatic Wing

**Type:** Ambassadorial Suites
**Phase:** Phase 2 (excavated interior)

Formal diplomatic quarters allocated for all sector factions: Federation, Kheth Confederacy, Varanid Authority, Fenric Identity, Salendi, Illari Sphere, Kelvari Reflection, and at least one additional independent world — whether occupied or not.

Staffing level is a live political signal. Full complement signals active engagement. Junior protest staff signals displeasure. A shuttered suite signals a crisis or a break.

---

## The Bar

**Type:** Social Hub / Entertainment
**Phase:** Phase 2 (excavated interior)

The main off-duty gathering point for outpost residents and visitors. More honest conversations happen here than in the briefing room. When the outgoing crew was replaced, this is where the standing crew waited with champagne and confetti — there is a tradition of watching the holodeck orientation monitors on new crew arrival day.

---

## RiMi's Entertarium

**Type:** Holosuite Complex / Restaurant / Bar
**Phase:** Phase 2 (excavated interior)

Named for its proprietors: Rima and Miki, a Bynar pair. The combined callsign RiMi is how the establishment is listed in the outpost directory. Neither will say which of them is Rima and which is Miki. Both answer to either name. To most humanoids they are visually indistinguishable.

Three services: holosuite time, a restaurant, and a bar. The holosuites are the premium draw — recreation, training scenarios, and full environmental simulations. The restaurant and bar are higher-end than the public Bar, catering to diplomats and visitors.

Outpost staff receive a monthly quota for holosuite time; unused quota does not roll over. Visitor access is pay-per-use. Custom programs are available on request, for the right price.

---

## Medical Bay

**Type:** Medical Facility
**Phase:** Phase 2 (excavated interior)

Open to all outpost residents and visitors. Staffed by Federation-trained medics; equipped with standard advanced medical technology. Treats both physical and psychological ailments — for a posting 85 days from the nearest proper hospital, the psychological component is significant.

Access to Surgery and the Isolation Ward is staff-controlled. Emergency fire doors can seal the bay from the public zone under certain alert conditions.

---

## Security Headquarters

**Type:** Security Operations Hub
**Phase:** Phase 2 (excavated interior)

The nerve center for outpost security: surveillance systems, holding cells, and the main armory. Staffed under civilian authority — Security operations are run by civilian charter under [[barry-smith]], not rotating Starfleet personnel.

Emergency fire doors can seal the facility from the public zone on certain alert conditions.

---

## Agriculture Facility

**Type:** Gardens / Food Production
**Phase:** Phase 2 (excavated interior; expanded during Dominion War)

Growing beds, hydroponic systems, and open walking space. Fresh food for the outpost, but that's almost incidental to its real function: it is the closest thing the outpost has to outside. Green, growing, alive — a counterpoint to rock and recycled air. Anyone can walk through; anyone can volunteer.

The facility was expanded during the Dominion War — more growing space, more plant variety, both as practical food contingency and as something deliberate to tend when the dispatches were bad. That expansion shows: the Dominion War section is slightly different in character from the original, planted with purpose, now fully mature.

---

## Persephone Agora

**Type:** Marketplace / Social Hub
**Phase:** Phase 2 (excavated interior — largest open space on the outpost)

The commercial and social heart of the outpost's civilian life. Loud, crowded during peak hours, and smells like six different cuisines simultaneously — which is considered a feature. Permanent shops, cafes, and stalls sit alongside transient traders who set up for a docking cycle and move on. Several restaurants and at least one additional bar occupy the Agora proper.

Small vendors operate wherever they can negotiate space — in the Agora, in adjacent corridors, occasionally in spots that are technically unauthorised until security notices. This is an ongoing low-grade negotiation. Nobody is particularly upset about it.

---

## Observation Deck

**Type:** Public Viewing / Science / Social Space
**Phase:** Tower (upper level, public-accessible)

The one part of the Tower that isn't staff-restricted. A panoramic view of the surrounding asteroid field, docking traffic, and open space. Three zones that coexist without friction: a working stellar science station (open to science staff and visiting researchers), a cocktail lounge (higher-end than the Bar, quieter), and meeting rooms along the inner wall for groups that want the view without the ambience.

---

## The Klingon Restaurant

**Type:** Restaurant / Bar
**Phase:** Phase 2 (Persephone Agora)

It has a name. Nobody uses it. Everyone calls it the Klingon restaurant.

The food is fresh — not replicated, cooked. The bloodwine is genuine. The furniture is heavy, scarred, and has survived everything that's been done to it. The cook is Klingon, male, gruff in a way that reads as quiet rather than hostile. He runs the kitchen himself with minimal staff and does not volunteer information about himself.

Rumors about his backstory circulate freely (spy, disgraced warrior, Gowron's personal chef, descendant of Kahless). When asked, he shrugs and says: *"vutwI' jIH neH."* I'm just a cook. The shrug does a lot of work.
